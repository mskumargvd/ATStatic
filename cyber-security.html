Cyber Security Professional Program

Your gateway to becoming a certified security professional, with a primary focus on SOC analysis.

Why Learn Cyber Security?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. This course provides a comprehensive foundation in modern defensive cyber security, focusing on the practical skills required to become a Security Operations Center (SOC) Analyst and protect organizations from real-world threats.

Career Paths in Cyber Security

For Freshers

Entry-level roles are a great way to get your foot in the door. They typically focus on monitoring, incident response, and vulnerability assessment.

  • SOC Analyst (Tier 1)
  • Cybersecurity Technician
  • IT Auditor
  • Incident & Intrusion Analyst

For Experienced Professionals

With experience, you can move into specialized and leadership roles that require deep technical expertise and strategic thinking.

  • Penetration Tester / Ethical Hacker
  • Security Architect
  • Cybersecurity Manager (CISO)
  • Threat Hunter

Primary Focus: SOC Analyst

This program is primarily designed to build a strong foundation for a career as a Security Operations Center (SOC) Analyst. A SOC Analyst is the first line of defense, responsible for monitoring and analyzing an organization's security posture on an ongoing basis. You will learn to detect, analyze, and respond to cybersecurity incidents using a combination of cutting-edge technology and a strong foundational knowledge of threats and vulnerabilities.

Course Curriculum

Month 1: The Defender's Foundation

  • Week 1-2: Security & Networking Fundamentals

    Intro to the CIA Triad, common threats (Malware, Phishing), and essential networking concepts (TCP/IP, OSI Model) for security analysis. Hands-on with Wireshark.

  • Week 3: Operating System Hardening

    Fundamentals of Windows & Linux security. Learn to harden systems by managing user permissions, access controls, and configuring firewalls.

  • Week 4: The Attacker's Mindset & Threat Intelligence

    Understand Indicators of Compromise (IoCs), TTPs, and the MITRE ATT&CK Framework to think like an adversary and anticipate threats.

Month 2: The Analyst's Toolkit

  • Week 5-6: SIEM & Log Analysis

    Master Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) tools like Splunk. Learn to write advanced queries and correlation rules to detect suspicious patterns in logs.

  • Week 7: Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR)

    Learn how EDR tools monitor endpoints for signs of compromise. Analyze alerts to trace the steps of a mock malware infection.

  • Week 8: Vulnerability Management

    Use tools like Nessus or OpenVAS to scan for vulnerabilities. Understand CVSS scoring to prioritize critical patches and misconfigurations.

Month 3: Incident Response & Capstone Project

  • Week 9: Phishing & Email Analysis

    Analyze suspicious emails, examine headers, and use sandboxing tools to safely inspect links and attachments.

  • Week 10: Introduction to Digital Forensics

    Learn the basics of evidence handling, chain of custody, and use tools like Autopsy to examine a disk image for hidden or deleted artifacts.

  • Week 11-12: Capstone: SOC Analyst Simulation

    Become a SOC Analyst for a day. Work through a series of real-world alert scenarios in a simulated environment, requiring you to investigate, document, and recommend remediation steps for each threat.
